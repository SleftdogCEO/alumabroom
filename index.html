The Problem

Your garage shouldn't look like this.

The average American home has 6-10 long-handled tools crammed into closets, garages, and laundry rooms. Each one does a single job, takes up the same space, and most are only used a few times a year.

The clutter adds up:

  • 6+ separate handles for 6 different jobs
  • Each tool takes 2-3 feet of vertical space
  • Broom closets overflow, garages are packed
  • RVs, apartments, and dorms have zero room
  • Every handle is the same — why own six?
  • Replacing one tool means buying a whole new handle

The Solution

Razor-Slide. Bolt-Lock. Done.

Inspired by how a razor blade seats into its handle — the aluminum flat bar slides into a precision channel on each tool head, then locks with two stainless steel wing nuts. 10-second swap. Zero tools required.

1

Slide In

The flat aluminum handle slides into the channel on any tool head — like a razor blade into its holder. Precision-fit, anti-rotation design.

2

Bolt Up

Two carriage bolts are pre-installed from the bottom of each tool head. Square necks prevent spinning. The bolts pass through slotted holes in the handle.

3

Wing-Nut Lock

Hand-tighten the two stainless steel wing nuts. Double-bolt clamping means rock-solid connection — no wobble, no slip, even under heavy load.

Engineering

Technical Design

Every tool head shares the same universal channel receiver. The only thing that changes is what's below the channel.

Anti-Rotation

The flat bar profile prevents the handle from twisting under load. Round tubes rotate — flat bars don't.

Standard Hardware

1/4"-20 carriage bolts and wing nuts. Lost a wing nut? Walk into any hardware store. No proprietary parts.

Graceful Failure

A loosening wing nut gives you a wobble warning. Plastic snap-locks fail suddenly — the head flies off with no warning.

Works Wet or Dirty

Metal threading doesn't jam with grit, dust, or water. Plastic clips and spring buttons seize up in real-world conditions.
